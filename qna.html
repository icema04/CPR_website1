Frequently Asked Questions

What is CPR?

CPR, or Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation, is a vital technique that keeps blood and oxygen flowing when someone’s heart has stopped or they’ve stopped breathing. It’s a crucial skill that saves lives!

When should CPR be performed?

If you see someone who’s unresponsive and not breathing normally, or only gasping, start CPR right away. Every second counts!

How can I learn CPR?

Get trained through certified courses by organizations like the American Heart Association or the Red Cross. Or explore online resources and practice with an instructor—it’s easier than you think and can truly save lives.
