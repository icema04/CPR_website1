Frequently Asked Questions

What is CPR?
CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) is an emergency procedure that combines chest compressions with rescue breaths to help restore blood circulation and breathing in someone whose heart has stopped.
When should I perform CPR?
CPR should be performed when a person is unresponsive and not breathing or only gasping. Always call emergency services first before starting CPR.
Can CPR save lives?
Yes, CPR can significantly increase the chance of survival in cardiac arrest cases if performed immediately and effectively.
Do I need formal training to do CPR?
While training is highly recommended, untrained individuals can still perform hands-only CPR (chest compressions only) in emergencies until help arrives.
What is hands-only CPR?
Hands-only CPR involves chest compressions without mouth-to-mouth breaths and is recommended for use by bystanders who are not trained in CPR.
